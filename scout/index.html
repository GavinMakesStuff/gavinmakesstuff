Getting started with Scout
Follow these steps to analyze postings, track applications, and stay organized.
1
Set up your profile
Click Edit profile in the sidebar to enter your background, target industry, salary, experience, and certifications. Or Upload resume to auto-fill everything.
2
Share your location (optional)
Click Share location in the sidebar so Scout can calculate driving distance to each job's work address. Entirely optional.
3
Paste job descriptions
Click Analyze posting in the middle column and paste any job description text. Use Add another to analyze several postings at once.
4
Review results
Each posting gets a 1–10 score with a written explanation. Click any card in the list to see the full detail on the right. Use the filter pills to focus on High, Partial, or Low matches.
5
Star, save, and apply
Use the quick action buttons at the bottom of each card to Star priority postings, Save to bookmark, or Apply to move it to your application tracker.
6
Track applications
In the Applied view, log date applied, follow up status, contact name, email, LinkedIn, posting URL, and notes for each application.
7
Find a contact
Click Find contact on any saved or applied card to search for a publicly listed HR or hiring manager email. Or enter one manually.
8
Copy resume keywords
Each card shows Hard Skills, Soft Skills, and Industry Terms extracted from the posting. Click any keyword to copy it, or use Copy all for the full list.
Tips for best results: Paste the complete job description — the more text Scout has, the more accurate the analysis. Fill in your years of experience and certifications in your profile, since these have the biggest effect on match scoring. All data is stored locally in your browser and persists between sessions.
