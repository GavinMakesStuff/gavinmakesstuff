Getting started with Scout
Follow these steps to analyze job postings, track applications, and stay organized.
1
Set up your profile
Click Edit profile to enter your background, target industry, salary expectation and currency, years of experience, certifications, and what kind of work you're looking for.

Optionally upload your resume (PDF or Word) to auto-fill your profile.
2
Share your location (optional)
Click 📍 Share my location so Scout can calculate the approximate driving distance from you to each job's work address. This is entirely optional.
3
Paste job descriptions
Copy the full text of any job posting and paste it into the text area. Click + Add another job posting to analyze several at once.
4
Analyze and review results
Click Analyze and watch the progress bar while Scout scores each posting out of 10, summarizes the role, checks benefits, reputation, and work location.

Use the filter bar to focus on High, Partial, or Low matches.
5
Highlight key postings
Click the ☆ star next to any job title to highlight it as a priority. Starred postings are saved automatically so the highlight has somewhere to live.
6
Save and track applications
Click ☆ Save to bookmark a job. Click ✉ Mark Applied to move it to your Applications tracker, where you can log the date applied, follow up status, notes, and a contact.
7
Find a contact and follow up
On any saved or applied posting, click 🔍 Find contact to search for an HR or hiring manager email, or enter one manually if you come across it elsewhere.
8
Grab resume keywords
Each card includes Hard Skills, Soft Skills, and Industry Terms. Click any keyword to copy it, or ⎘ Copy all for the full list.
💡
Tips for best results: Paste the complete job description, the more text Scout has, the more accurate the analysis. Fill in your years of experience and certifications, since these have the biggest effect on match scoring accuracy. Everything you enter stays stored locally in your browser and persists between sessions.
